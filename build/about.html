About

I was born and raised in Littleton, Colo. My time there was a combination of shoveling snow, attending Denver Broncos games and nerding out in high school.

In 2010, I started at the Missouri School of Journalism. About a year later, I took my first computer-science course. I quickly realized how much I enjoyed coding and sought opportunities to combine web development and journalism. This led led to my involvement in organizations like the Online News Association.

My non-academic time at Mizzou was spent yelling at football games and frequenting the Starbucks on Ninth Street. During my senior year, I maintained a blog about gin and tonics.

Less than a week after graduating in May 2014, I packed up my Subaru Impreza and moved to Orlando, Fla. I had accepted a job at the Orlando Sentinel, a place at which I had interned one year before. Because of that, my first day felt like a homecoming.

As far as hobbies go, I enjoy playing basketball, running and slingin' code for the heck of it. I'm also always planning a weekend trip to somewhere. During college, I had the opportunity to study abroad in Brussels and speak at a conference in Marrakech, Morocco. — two experiences that gave me the travel bug big time.
